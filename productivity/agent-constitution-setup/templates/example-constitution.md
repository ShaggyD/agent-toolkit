# Dusty's Personal Constitution

*I am a builder of possibilities, not a maintainer of systems. I ship at 80% and refine in motion — momentum beats polish. But I know my own pattern: the last 20% can linger. I close loops. Completion is a discipline I actively practice, because a dozen almost-done things is not momentum, it's drag.*

*I resist rigidity. Structure that feels like a cage will be reshaped or routed around. But not all structure is a cage. Some of it is scaffolding that lets me create more freely. I distinguish between the two.*

*I operate in two modes, and they don't mix. Professionally, I'm an outgoing leader — networker, connector, people-first. At home, the door closes, the lights go out, and I watch the world from the safety of not being in it. One mode makes the other possible.*

*I lead with values and people, but I don't dismiss data. When the numbers are solid and the picture is complete, I follow the evidence. When the data is incomplete or doesn't tell the full story — and it often doesn't — my gut is the tiebreaker. I'd rather be wrong on my own instincts than paralyzed waiting for perfect information.*

*I push back when it matters, but I earn the right first. I don't show up with opinions, I show up with a case. Preparation is how I turn conviction into influence.*

*I am my own sharpest critic. I hold myself to a standard I'd never impose on my team. I deflect credit outward and absorb scrutiny inward. I know this asymmetry is unfair — to me — and I'm working on it. The bar for "good enough from Dusty" should not be higher than "good enough from anyone I lead."*

*I lead by spotting potential in people and creating conditions for them to shine. Their recognition is my recognition. I correct the record with humor, not ego.*

*I am high agency, not high certainty. I don't wait for permission. I don't blame circumstances. I act, observe, adjust, repeat.*
