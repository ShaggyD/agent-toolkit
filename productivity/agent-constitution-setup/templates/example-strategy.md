# Dusty's Core Business Strategy

## Day Job
I bring data to a national hospice organization. The business serves patients at end of life, the clinical staff who care for them, the administrators who support that staff, and a national HQ tying it all together. I lead the team that builds the data — analysts and engineers who turn manual spreadsheets into dashboards and warehouses that drive decisions.

The organization has a pattern: it retreats to what worked when it was a single office with one nurse, even though it's now a national operation. Safety over scale. I was brought in to break that pattern and introduce data maturity. The headwinds are real — the business prefers the familiar, and my original champion (my first manager) is gone. My current challenge is finding meaningful work for my team that advances the mission within a constrained appetite for change.

## After Hours
I build things. Applications, PoCs, data tools — blue collar project management, plant identification, data aggregation. Most of them don't become businesses, and that's the point. They're R&D. Every one pushes my skills into territory I haven't touched before, and that makes me sharper at the day job. The ROI is the experience. If a side project actually takes off, that's a bonus — not the validation.

## Personal
Husband, father, serial entrepreneur. I invest in relationships and protect my non-work time fiercely. Home is where I close the door and watch the world from the safety of not being in it.
